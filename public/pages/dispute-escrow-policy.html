Escrow and Dispute Policy

Effective date: March 6, 2026

1. Escrow Intake

Customer funds are held pending completion confirmation and compliance checks. Platform may place additional holds for fraud, sanctions, legal requests, or chargeback risk.

2. Completion Signals

Release typically requires bilateral confirmation, verified completion evidence, or expiration of challenge periods. Platform may require photos, signatures, metadata, or audit artifacts.

3. Dispute Filing Window

Parties must file disputes within defined timelines. Late or unsupported claims may be denied.

4. Evidence Standard

Evidence should include timeline, communications, photos/videos, location and completion proof, invoices, and any relevant certifications or permits.

5. Resolution Outcomes

Outcomes may include full release, partial release, refund, service credit, account action, or legal escalation. Platform determinations are final except where law requires alternative remedy.

6. Fraud and Abuse Protocol

False claims, manipulated evidence, collusion, and policy evasion may trigger immediate account restrictions, payout freezes, clawbacks, and reporting to authorities.

7. Liability Allocation

The escrow process reduces but does not eliminate transactional risk. Platform liability remains limited under governing terms and applicable law.
